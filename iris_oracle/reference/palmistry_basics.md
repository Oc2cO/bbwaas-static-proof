# Palmistry Basics (Chiromancy)

## Overview

Palmistry, or chiromancy, interprets the lines, mounts, shapes and finger features of the hands as indicators of personality traits and life themes.  Practitioners distinguish the **non‑dominant hand** (natural tendencies and potential) from the **dominant hand** (how those traits manifest in life).  The practice is not scientifically validated, so readings are meant as reflective storytelling rather than prediction.

## Mounts and Hand Shapes

The fleshy pads at the base of each finger and along the palm are called **mounts**.  Each mount is associated with planetary energy and specific qualities【310429449045207†L290-L347】:

- **Mount of Jupiter (index finger):** Leadership, ambition and self‑confidence.
- **Mount of Saturn (middle finger):** Responsibility, discipline and seriousness.
- **Mount of Apollo (ring finger):** Creativity, charisma and appreciation for beauty.
- **Mount of Mercury (little finger):** Communication, business and adaptability.
- **Mount of Luna (outer palm opposite the thumb):** Imagination, intuition and emotions.
- **Mount of Mars (divided into inner and outer mounts):** Courage, drive and resilience; the inner mount (beneath Mars and Jupiter) reflects mental courage, while the outer mount reflects physical energy.
- **Mount of Venus (base of the thumb):** Love, passion and sensuality.

Hand shapes are often linked to the four classical elements【310429449045207†L349-L436】:

- **Fire hands:** Square palms with short fingers; suggest leadership, enthusiasm and impulsiveness.
- **Earth hands:** Square or rectangular palms with short fingers; associated with practicality, reliability and endurance.
- **Air hands:** Square or rectangular palms with long fingers; linked to intellect, communication and curiosity.
- **Water hands:** Oval palms with long, slender fingers; reflect sensitivity, empathy and imagination.

Finger attributes also carry meaning.  Long fingers denote patience and attention to detail; short fingers suggest impatience or decisiveness; flexible thumbs indicate adaptability, while stiff thumbs are read as stubbornness【310429449045207†L349-L436】.

## Reading Template

Each palm reading follows a four‑section structure:

1. **What the Oracle Sees** – Describe the lines, mounts, finger shapes and overall hand type.  Note whether lines are long, short, deep, faint, broken or doubled; observe which mounts are pronounced.
2. **What This Reveals** – Interpret these features using palmistry traditions.  Relate line qualities to vitality, intellect, emotional life or life path; connect mount prominence to personality traits and strengths.
3. **A Whisper** – Offer gentle guidance or reflection.  Encourage the seeker to harness their natural strengths or to balance over‑emphasis with mindfulness.  Avoid deterministic statements.
4. **Look Ahead** – Suggest an uplifting possibility or area of growth.  Highlight potential opportunities or changes implied by the lines.
